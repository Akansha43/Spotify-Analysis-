#  🎵 Spotify Data Analytics Project 
This project analyzes **Spotify listening patterns** using albums, artists, and track-level insights.   Built as part of a data visualization project using Power BI.

## 🎯 Skills Demonstrated
- **Data Collection & Cleaning** – Ensured dataset quality and consistency.  
- **Data Modeling** – Structured data for meaningful analysis.  
- **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** – Identified key trends and outliers.  
- **Data Visualization** – Built interactive dashboards to communicate findings.  
- **Business Insights & Storytelling** – Translated raw data into clear insights for decision-making.  
- **Tools Used** – SQL, Excel, Python (Pandas), Power BI/Looker Studio, DAX.  

## 📌 Business Objectives
- Track **albums, artists, and tracks** played over time.  
- Compare **Latest Year (LY) vs Previous Year (PY)** with YoY growth trends.  
- Identify **weekday vs weekend** listening behavior.  
- Discover **top performers** (Top 5 Albums, Artists, and Tracks).  
- Analyze **peak listening hours** with a heatmap.  
- Provide **drill-down & drill-through functionality** for deeper insights.  
## 📊 Key Analysis
### Albums
- Total Albums Played Over Time  
- Albums per Year (Min & Max)  
- Top 5 Albums  

### Artists
- Artist Diversity by Year  
- Artist Listening Trends Over Time  
- Top 5 Artists  

### Tracks
- Track Diversity by Year  
- Most Played Tracks  
- Year-over-Year Trends  

### Listening Patterns
- **Listening Hours Heatmap** – Peak times across weekdays/weekends.  
- **Quadrant Analysis** – High/Low frequency vs listening time (engagement mapping).  

### Details Grid
- Drillable view of Albums, Artists, Tracks.  
- Export to CSV for reporting needs.
- 
## 📈 Insights Generated
- Weekends had **20–25% higher listening activity** than weekdays.  
- **Latest Year vs Previous Year** analysis showed positive YoY growth in engagement.  
- A few niche tracks had **low frequency but high listening time**, indicating strong fan dedication.  
- Top 5 Artists contributed disproportionately to overall listening volume.
